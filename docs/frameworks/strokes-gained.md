Strokes Gained (SG) is a comprehensive golf analytics system that measures performance by comparing every shot taken against a defined baseline (typically a PGA Tour player or a specific handicap benchmark). It determines whether a shot was better or worse than the average, allowing golfers to identify exactly which areas of their game (tee shots, approaches, short game, or putting) are responsible for their score.

3. Key Categories of Strokes Gained
Strokes Gained is broken down into four main facets of the game, as described by Shot Scope and BreakXGolf: 
Off the Tee (SG: OTT): Measures performance on all tee shots on par 4s and 5s.
Approach the Green (SG: APP): Measures shots designed to reach the green (including tee shots on par 3s).
Around the Green (SG: ATG): Measures chips, pitches, and bunker shots.
Putting (SG: Putting): Measures all putts, comparing them to the average putts taken from that distance.

4. Interpretation
Positive Number (+): You performed better than the average benchmark (e.g., +0.5 means you gained half a stroke).
Negative Number (-): You performed worse than the average benchmark.
Zero (0): You performed exactly at the average.

5. Benefits for Amateur Golfers
While originally for pros, apps like Shot Scope and Arccos now enable amateurs to compare their stats against different handicap benchmarks (e.g., 5, 10, 15, 20 handicap) rather than just the PGA Tour. This helps identify specific weaknesses, such as realizing that poor putting is costing them 3 strokes per round, while their driving is actually above average for their handicap level.
