The Scoring Method, developed by Will Robins, is a comprehensive course management and mental strategy system designed to lower golf scores without requiring significant swing changes. It shifts the focus from traditional, aggressive, and often reckless play to a "two-game" approach focusing on "Entering the Scoring Zone" (ESZ) and "Down in Three" (DSZ).

Entering the Scoring Zone (ESZ): The first goal is to get the ball within 100 yards of the hole in a set number of shots based on skill level (usually in regulation).
Down in Three (DSZ): Once inside 100 yards, the goal is to get the ball into the hole in three strokes or less.

Key Strategies and Principles
100-Yard Focus: The primary objective is to reach the 100-yard marker efficiently. Even if it takes three shots to get there, the player treats the remaining 100 yards as a new hole, focusing on a "down in three" (wedge + 2 putts) to salvage a bogey.
Avoiding Penalty Shots: The method prioritizes keeping the ball in play over distance, aiming for 80+ percent accuracy.
"Get Out of Trouble" Philosophy: When in trouble, the focus is to play it safe and get back into play on the first attempt, rather than attempting a risky "hero shot" that could lead to a blow-up hole.
Short Game and Putting: The system emphasizes mastering putts within 4 feet and managing lag-putting speed over reading breaks.

Purposeful Practice
The statistics gathered during the round via The Scoring Method are transferred to a "Purposeful Practice Card", which acts as a guide to determine which areas of the game need improvement. This turns raw, chaotic practice into a targeted, efficient routine.
